Rotations only change the facing direction; translations only change the position.
Apply each transformation in sequence. Axes and clockwise directions are defined as follows:
  Y axis - vertical axis. Clockwise viewed from above: north→east→south→west.
  X axis - left/right axis. Clockwise viewed from the right: north→up→south→down.
  Z axis - forward/backward axis. Clockwise viewed from the front: up→east→down→west.
Counterclockwise is the reverse of clockwise in each case.
Positions are given as (x, y, z) coordinate triples. Positive x is to the right, positive y is upward, positive z is toward the viewer.
For relative position questions: choose the direction of the dominant axis (largest absolute difference from start position). If two axes are tied, discard and regenerate.
